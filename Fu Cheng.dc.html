Urban Design — Public Realm

Fu Cheng

SiteZhongzheng Rd & the Canal, Tainan
Year2023–2024
RoleIndividual studio project
Concept

"府" refers to the ancient name of Tainan. "埕" refers to a traditional Taiwanese family courtyard — a place where children play and adults farm, and where both formal and informal activities can unfold.

This project introduces the idea of a resilient city by transforming key public spaces into urban water retention grounds. Below ground, a demonstrative urban reservoir would help address Tainan's recurring annual water shortages. During droughts, it could serve as an emergency water source for the public, while in normal times it remains self-sufficient. Open to the public, the facility also invites people to learn about water resource use, serving an educational purpose.

At ground level, a range of everyday functions would be reintroduced to the site. These include government offices for civic affairs, commercial uses, and tourism-related activities. Together, they respond to the scale of local street life and return public space to its citizens. As an urban water reservoir, the facility helps promote sustainable value, revitalize local industry, and become part of the "lifeblood" of Tainan.
