Venice Architecture Biennale — Taiwan Pavilion

Climate Justice

SiteVenice, Italy
Year2024–2025
Role19th Venice Architecture Biennale — Taiwan Pavilion
Concept

Taiwan's urban centers are currently facing a critical urban heat island (UHI) challenge, evidenced by record-breaking temperatures in Taipei reaching 39.7°C in 2020 and a persistent temperature gap of 3°C to 5°C compared to suburban areas caused by impermeable surfaces, concrete structures, and artificial heat sources. To address this, Professor Lin Tzu-Ping and the Building and Climate Research Lab (BCLab) at NCKU developed the HiSAN network to provide real-time monitoring across six cities, while also utilizing TReAD historical data to map urban heat environments and support early warning systems. Research into socio-economic indicators — such as population age, income, and healthcare — reveals a significant environmental injustice, where high vulnerability areas like Wan Hua District face extreme heat risks despite their residents contributing less to environmental impact through lower car usage, whereas wealthier districts like Da An area show higher adaptation capacity. These science approaches aim to foster collaboration between the public and private sectors to enhance urban resilience and mitigate the multi-faceted impacts of the heat island effect on public health and energy consumption.

策展人 Curators: 林子平 / 李思誼
