CKS Memorial Park — Urban Speculation

Democratize.exe

SiteChiang Kai-shek Memorial Park, Taipei
Year2022
RoleIndividual studio project
#Democratic Transition #Data Analysis #Problem Discussion #Public Governance

Taiwan's progress toward democratization has been marked by the concerted efforts of many pioneers. However, nowadays, facing the problems of information asymmetry, media bias, and the spreading of fake news, our democratic society demands action to address the dysfunctional crisis.

Our project intends to transform Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall into a place where "realization of popular sovereignty" is formed. By providing real-time data and governance outcomes, our project provides a foundation where the public can discuss, supervise, and make decisions. From the era of passive authoritarian government to the present day of active and democratic government, Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall will be transformed to return sovereignty to the people to achieve democratic transformation.

The Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall will be converted into a big national data center to collect, analyze, and visualize data. The building will be deconstructed, except for the 1st and 2nd floors, which will be used as office space. The rest of the building materials will be scattered all over and integrated into the newly constructed landscape buildings. In addition, the geodesic buildings that integrate the old structures will present a variety of data according to the theme, and at the same time combine with corresponding functional leisure spaces to encourage the target groups to utilize them. Furthermore, conduct civic discussions and social experiments on the key issues presented by the data. Finally, the results will be exported and applied to all parts of Taiwan. Represented by our title, exe, we will be the main source of executing and experimenting with a new generation of transformational justice and democracy.

The five landmark buildings will address all ages, environmental, industrial, residential, and educational issues. Our five landmark buildings each focus on the following categories. (1) Ideal living space for all ages: We will create an inclusive playground where all age groups can enjoy recreation and leisure space and provide an opportunity to discuss policies on early childhood care, youth employment, and elderly care, to achieve the good of all ages. (2) Create resilient households: We plan to build a bathhouse that collects reusable rainwater and heats it with solar energy. This offers new possibilities for interaction between people and nature and discusses common environmental crises in a bathhouse that symbolizes the removal of classes and social hierarchy. (3) Industrial blockchain: A café will be set up for businesses and cooperations from different industries to collaborate, further allowing them to discuss possible partnerships and make suggestions on future policy directions. (4) Beyond academics: We plan to create an exhibition space that will go beyond the traditional classroom learning experience for humanities and arts courses, and facilitate the exchange of educational results in various fields. (5) Housing and Equal Rights Sharing Ticket: We decide to build a "Disappearing Water Wall", in which the interactive installation empowers interested parties to temporarily draw their dream houses on the water wall and allow ideas to collide. By painting people’s ideal housing units, we can perform housing justice, additionally, receive feedback and data on our solution. Empowered by discussion and democratic practices of the public, our landscape represents the pioneer of a modern solution to social transformative justice. By creating a successful model, we will be expanding the social influences, and accomplishing proactive public governance, furthermore, strengthening the democratization of Taiwan as we build our way to achieve democracy.
